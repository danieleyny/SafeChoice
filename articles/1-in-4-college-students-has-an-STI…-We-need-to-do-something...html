1 in 4 college students has an STI… We need to do something.

College life can be a party, but it’s also full of new and exciting social opportunities. Unfortunately, it can also be a breeding ground for serious health risks.
However, did you know:

“One in four college students has a sexually transmitted infection (STI) - this may be higher than you expected. STIs are infections transmitted by intimate sexual contact.“

Washington and Lee University

This means that even though you only have to worry about one person, you might still get one yourself. And if you don’t want to end up with a case of genital herpes or chlamydia You should know how to protect yourself. The best way to protect yourself from STIs is by using condoms. If you’re not sure how to use them, ask your doctor or a nurse for advice; they’ll show you how and help you get started on a good routine. You should also take an STI test if you have sex with someone new or if you experience symptoms like itching, burning, or genital discharge.

The first rule of STI prevention is the same as the first rule of life: know your status. If you’re sexually active and not sure if you have an STI, get tested. Your doctor or a local clinic can run some tests for free.

Some Prevention Tips

  1. Abstinence, or refraining from sexual activity, is the only 100% effective way to prevent STDs.
  2. Consistent and correct use of condoms during sexual activity can greatly reduce the risk of contracting an STD. Make sure to use a new condom every time you have sex, and check the expiration date.
  3. It's important to get tested regularly for STDs, especially if you are sexually active. This can help you detect any infections early and get prompt treatment if necessary.
  4. The more sexual partners you have, the greater your risk of contracting an STD. Limiting your number of sexual partners can help reduce your risk.
  5. It's important to have open and honest communication with your sexual partners about your sexual health and history. This can help you both make informed decisions about sexual activity and reduce the risk of contracting an STD.
  6. Vaccines are available for certain STDs, such as HPV and hepatitis B. Getting vaccinated can greatly reduce your risk of contracting these infections.
  7. Avoid sharing personal items like towels or razors that could potentially transmit STDs.
